Simple Definition: Core stability is the ability to keep the trunk strong and controlled while the arms and legs move. For William, it helps him stay balanced in contact, jump stronger, sprint cleaner, change direction, and protect his lower back.

What Core Stability Is, And What It Is Not

This is not about doing hundreds of sit-ups. It is about building a strong, stable platform so William can run, jump, defend, and absorb contact without losing posture.

Workout B
Contact, Groin, And Anti-Rotation Strength
Use this when he has time and feels fresh. Do not force it if legs are heavy.
10–15 Minutes
ExerciseSets / RepsHow To Do ItWhy It Helps
Pallof Press3 × 12 each sideBand or cable at chest height. Press straight out and resist rotation.Helps him stay strong when bumped or pulled off balance.
Copenhagen Plank3 × 20 sec each sideTop leg on bench, hold side plank position. Start with knee-supported version if needed.Builds groin/adductor strength for cutting, tackling, and lateral defending.
Hanging Knee Raises3 × 10Hang from bar, raise knees under control, no swinging.Builds lower-ab and hip-flexor control.
Farmer Carries3 × 40 yardsCarry heavy dumbbells, tall posture, slow controlled steps.Improves bracing, posture, grip, and contact strength.

Safety Rules
How To Know If The Core Work Is Too Much
Core work should make him stronger, not sore and slow.
Stop If Needed
Red FlagWhat It MeansWhat To Do
Lower back painHe is losing position or overusing the back.Stop, reduce time, and use easier versions.
Groin sorenessCopenhagen plank or lateral work may be too aggressive.Skip Copenhagen plank for 3–5 days.
Hip flexor tightnessToo much knee raise work or fatigue from running.Reduce hanging knee raises and stretch hip flexors.
Form breaksThe set has gone too long.End the set. Quality beats time.
Heavy legs before matchTotal load is too high.Skip core or do only light mobility.

Simple Rule For William

If he finishes core work feeling taller, tighter, and more controlled, it was the right amount. If he finishes sore, tight, or tired, it was too much.
